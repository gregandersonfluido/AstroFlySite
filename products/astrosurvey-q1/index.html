AstroSurvey Q1 Geospatial Mapping Drone

"Advanced Geospatial Mapping for Precision Analysis"

Product Summary

The AstroSurvey Q1 Geospatial Mapping Drone is engineered for high-precision aerial surveying and mapping applications. Integrating multispectral imaging, LiDAR technology, and precision positioning systems, the AstroSurvey Q1 delivers centimeter-level accuracy for detailed terrain analysis, volumetric calculations, and environmental monitoring. Designed for surveying professionals, urban planners, agricultural specialists, and environmental researchers, it combines long flight duration with sophisticated data processing to transform complex geospatial data into actionable insights.

Detailed Specifications

Imaging & Sensors

Multispectral Camera System

  • High-Resolution RGB: 42MP global shutter sensor for detailed visual imagery
  • Multispectral Bands: Red, Green, Blue, Red Edge, Near Infrared (NIR) with individual calibration
  • Thermal Imaging: Optional thermal sensor for heat distribution mapping
  • Radiometric Calibration: Built-in calibration panel for accurate spectral analysis

LiDAR System

  • Point Cloud Density: Up to 600,000 points per second
  • Range: Effective mapping up to 200m altitude with multiple return capability
  • Accuracy: ±3cm vertical and horizontal accuracy under optimal conditions
  • Pattern: 360° scanning pattern for comprehensive terrain modeling

Positioning System

  • GNSS: Multi-constellation (GPS, GLONASS, Galileo, BeiDou) for global operation
  • RTK/PPK: Real-time kinematic and post-processed kinematic capabilities
  • Base Station Compatibility: Works with industry-standard correction services
  • Inertial Measurement: High-precision IMU for accurate positional data even in GPS-challenged environments

Support & Resources

Technical FAQs

Q1: What is the typical accuracy of the survey results?

A1: The AstroSurvey Q1 delivers horizontal and vertical accuracy of ±3cm with RTK enabled and proper ground control point usage. Accuracy can vary based on flight height, environmental conditions, and the quality of correction signals.

Q2: How are the multispectral bands calibrated?

A2: The system includes a calibrated reflectance panel for pre-flight calibration. Additionally, automatic radiometric calibration is performed during processing using sunlight sensors to compensate for varying light conditions during the survey.

Q3: What file formats are supported for export?

A3: The processing software supports multiple industry-standard formats including GeoTIFF, LAS/LAZ, XYZ point clouds, DXF, SHP, KML, and mesh formats such as OBJ and 3DS for 3D models.
